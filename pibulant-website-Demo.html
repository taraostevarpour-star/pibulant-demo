The Science

Biomarker Detection

Four signals.
Complete picture.

Biomarker 01
Skin pH
Measured via electrochemical detection. pH directly governs barrier function, microbial balance, and enzyme activity. A 0.5 deviation from optimal pH significantly alters ingredient absorption.
Method: Potentiometric electrochemical · Range: 4.0–7.0
Biomarker 02
Sebum Level
Measured via optical lipid detection. Sebum overproduction is driven by SRD5A1 gene activity and directly informs whether oil-controlling or barrier-restoring formulas are needed.
Method: Optical lipid spectroscopy · Unit: μg/cm²
Biomarker 03
Melanin Index
Measured via spectrophotometric reflectance at 660nm and 940nm LED wavelengths. Correlates with tyrosinase enzyme activity and MC1R gene expression.
Method: Dual-wavelength spectrophotometry · LED: 660nm / 940nm
Biomarker 04
Sensitivity Score
Derived from electrochemical impedance measuring transepidermal water loss proxy, combined with questionnaire-weighted inputs. Maps to cytokine pathway tendency (IL-6, IL-1β).
Method: Impedance spectroscopy + questionnaire · Scale: 0–100
AI Formulation Engine
1

Biomarker normalization

Raw sensor readings normalized against population baselines and cross-validated between measurement sites.

2

Genetic tendency mapping

Values cross-referenced against gene-biomarker correlations. Six genetic pathways scored: FLG, MC1R/TYR, IL-6, SRD5A1, COL1A1, NRF2.

3

Active ingredient scoring

Each active ingredient is scored against all six pathway outputs. Final formula selects top-scoring ingredients with calibrated concentrations.

4

Safety constraint validation

Concentration limits, ingredient interactions, and sensitivity thresholds validated before formula is finalized.

5

Product mapping

Formula mapped to small-batch product recommendations (10–20ml) from the PibuLant Iranian botanical line.

Our Story

Skincare built on
biology, not categories.

"Skincare should not be based on skin type categories. It should be based on each person's measurable biology."

PibuLant was founded by Tara Ostovarpour, a Plant Genetic Engineer and R&D Scientist based in Seoul, Gangnam-gu. Her background in plant genetics gave her a precise understanding of active compounds in botanicals — and why standardized skincare formulas rarely work the way they should.

The problem Tara set out to solve is both simple and massive: every year, a significant portion of all skincare products purchased are thrown away — wrong formula, no effect, sensitivity reaction. The waste is enormous. And the solution isn't just "more personalization" — it's a fundamentally different production model.

PibuLant's approach: use AI photo analysis to understand each person's skin without any hardware, generate a precise formula using Iranian botanical actives and K-beauty biotechnology, and produce it in small batches through partner labs only when ordered. No pre-made stock. No surplus. Only what the skin actually needs, in the amount it needs.

The ingredient layer draws from Iran's botanical heritage — Saffron from Kurdistan, Licorice from Isfahan, Thyme from the Zagros mountains, Rosehip from the Alborz foothills. Each botanical is scientifically validated for its specific active role and blended with Korean biotechnology bases.

PibuLant is currently in Phase 1 — the AI web application MVP is live and being validated with real users. Phase 2 (small-batch product line + partner labs) follows as the platform grows. Tara is currently presenting PibuLant to the OASIS 5th Cohort accelerator program in Seoul and applying to the 2026 Global Startup Commercialization Support Program (KISED/Ministry of SMEs).
